Section 02

The AI Skills Divide

When you split employment data by AI proficiency, the picture diverges sharply. AI-skilled workers are holding near-full employment at approximately 97%, while non-AI workers are tracking closer to 89% — a gap that has widened every quarter since generative AI went mainstream.

56%
AI Wage Premium (2024)
Workers with AI skills earn 56% more than peers without them — a figure that doubled from 25% in a single year. PwC analyzed close to a billion job ads across six continents to reach this finding.

On the hiring side, AI and machine learning hiring grew 88% year-over-year in 2025. Meanwhile, administrative role hiring dropped 35.5%, and entry-level hiring collapsed by 73.4%. The jobs AI is displacing aren't hypothetical — they're the ones that used to be the first rung on the career ladder.

AI skills now carry a higher salary premium than a Master's degree in the UK labor market — 23% vs 13%. Skills-based hiring is overtaking credentialism in real time.

Sources: PwC Global AI Jobs Barometer 2025, Ravio 2026 Compensation Trends, WEF

Section 06

What To Do With This Data

For Job Seekers

Prioritize AI literacy immediately — even prompt engineering and AI-assisted workflows carry measurable salary premiums. Target healthcare, cybersecurity, and clean energy for recession-resistant career paths. Document AI project outcomes over credentials; practical experience carries 19–23% more value than certifications alone.

For Career Changers

The highest premiums go to professionals who bridge traditional expertise with AI capabilities. A marketing professional who understands customer psychology and AI-powered personalization becomes exceptionally valuable. AI skills can offset age and education disadvantages in hiring.

For Employers

Budget for AI talent premiums ($160K+ median). Invest in workforce upskilling — 77% of employers already plan to, but the gap between plan and execution is wide. Address the entry-level pipeline crisis: with hiring down 73%, succession planning needs urgent attention.
